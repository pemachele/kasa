]]> Are you wondering if you can immigrate to Canada with a criminal record? A criminal record can exclude you from gaining permanent residence in Canada. Fortunately, you are not alone and there’s a way out of this.

Canada wants new permanent residents who can apply as skilled workers through Express Entry, however, there are strict rules laid out for people with criminal records for security purposes. In fact, as of 2018, a driving under the influence (DUI) conviction could easily make a potential migrant inadmissible to Canada forever for severe criminality. However, there is a way out and that’s through the Criminal Rehabilitation (CR) application.

What is Criminal Rehabilitation?

Criminal Rehabilitation is a process devised to wipe your criminal record to make you admissible into Canada. It keeps your record permanently clean unless of course, you commit another crime.

Canada translates a person’s foreign criminal history into Canada’s equivalent to begin to deal with it. It could be translated based on serious and non-serious criminal offences, the processing fee costing CAD 1000 and CAD 200, respectively. Either way, you must be considered rehabilitated to receive your Canadian PR status.

However, you could have the option to apply for the Temporary Resident Permit (TRP) instead of the CR. While CR permanently clears your record and your TRP must be renewed else, you are still criminally inadmissible to Canada every time your TRP expires.

Meanwhile, CR is only obtainable for convictions outside of Canada. Otherwise, you may have to strive for a pardon from the Parole Board of Canada.

]]> Are you wondering when you can enter the Express Entry pool? You’re definitely not the only one. There are many others as Canada is looking to welcome new immigrants. Express Entry is the most popular and quickest immigration pathway for individuals who desire to move to Canada to gain Permanent Residence (PR).

Canada plans to welcome more than 400,000 new immigrants every year. This was authorized under its 2021-2023 Immigration Levels Plan aimed at supporting its post-Covid economic recovery.

Out of the over 400,000 new immigrants moving to Canada for permanent residence, 60% will go as skilled workers and Express Entry is the main and fastest way skilled worker applications are processed in Canada.

When to Enter the Express Entry Pool

Most newly arrived in Canada successfully migrated through Express Entry via the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP). To know when to enter the Express Entry pool, here are some things to do:

Find Out if You are Eligible for Canadian Immigration

Before you begin to apply, you must have read all the necessary requirements to be eligible for the FSW program. Find out if you are eligible.

Prior to the coronavirus pandemic, it was recorded that nearly half of the Express Entry immigrants were made up by the FSW program candidates. The other half came through the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP), and the Canadian Experience Class (CEC). According to CIC news, the IRCC has dispensed Express Entry invitations to PNP and CEC candidates since 2021. As a result, many FSWP candidates are waiting in the Express Entry pool. 

Potential FSWP candidates might think it reasonable to wait for the IRCC to resume issuing Invitations to Apply (ITA) before they get into the pool. However, this would be a mistake. You can get in if you discover you are eligible.

Calculate Your Express Entry Score

Although it’s safe to enter the pool now, the only risk would be your official language test expiring within two years of completing the test if you do not receive an ITA.

It is a minimal risk compared to the reward of obtaining a Canadian PR, so, why not take the significantly rewarding risk?

At the risk of your language test expiring, you stand a higher chance of obtaining your PR when you enter the Express Entry pool than you do waiting for IRCC to resume issuing ITA.

Why You Should Enter the Express Entry Pool

You Won’t Be Caught Unaware

It is not certain when IRCC will resume issuing ITAs to FSW program candidates. The Express Entry pool is quite competitive and you don’t want to be caught unawares when the issuing of ITAs is resumed. 

You Receive an Additional 600 CRS points

You stand a chance of receiving a PNP invitation too. Accepting the invitation gives you an additional 600 CRS points which can guarantee an Express Entry ITA.

Provinces in Canada annually invite Express Entry candidates who meet their province’s labour market needs to apply to their PNP. Candidates who receive provincial nominations can apply to receive a provincial nomination certificate. Afterwards, they inform the IRCC to receive their additional 600 CRS points which increases their chances of receiving an ITA for PR in the next Express Entry draw that targets PNP candidates.

You can Create a Plus Account

Being in the Express Entry pool allows you to create a Plus Account in the Canadian government’s Job Bank. This access avails you of suitable employment opportunities and you could receive a job offer that will give you an additional 50 or 200 CRS points. Although these points do not outrightly get you an ITA, however, it increases your chances of getting it. 

While you expect IRCC to resume issuing ITAs, it’s okay to sit on the fence about entering the Express Entry, however, it will be in your favour to do so. The Express Entry pool simply places you one step closer to obtaining your ITA for your PR in Canada.

]]> Did you know that the Canadian passport is one of the most powerful passports in the world? Some passports are deemed more powerful than others. However, the strongest passports are the ones that offer the most outstanding travel freedom to travellers. In this article, you will understand the benefits of a Canadian passport.

While choosing travel destinations, travellers mostly consider destinations they can go to, what documents would be required before entry and the cost. Therefore, the world’s strongest passports are the ones that easily allow travellers into many countries without supplementary entry requirements like the visa.

Why the Canadian Passport is One of the Most Powerful

According to Henley Passport Index, a passport is ranked highly if it allows the holders entry into many countries with either of:

  • Passport
  • Visa on arrival
  • Electronic travel authorization (eTA)/ online visa waiver.

Using this criterion, Canadian passports are ranked in the 5th group of countries along with the United States, the United Kingdom, Malta, Greece, France, Hungary, Czech Republic, and Poland.

The Canadian passport is ranked 9th place in the most powerful passports in the world according to the Guide Passport Ranking Index. This means that Canadian passport holders can travel to many world countries visa-free. Out of the existing 195 countries in the world, this report shows that a Canadian passport holder can travel to 188 of them without a visa through passport only, a visa on arrival, or via an eTA.

In addition to providing free access to over 180 countries in the world, the Canadian passport is one of the most desirable passports in the world. The holders have free visa access and visa on arrival into countries such as:

  • The United Kingdom
  • The United States
  • United Arab Emirates
  • Japan
  • European Union (the entire EU), etc.

It allows for instant worldwide travel opportunities for its holders. 

When entering Canada, holders of visa-free passports are required to obtain beforehand, an Electronic Travel Authorization. It costs $7CAD and it can be obtained online. 

Meanwhile, when flying into a country without visa restrictions, Canadian passport holders will likely be required to show their passports and some other documents such as:

  • International travel insurance
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Proof of sufficient financial means
  • Countries such as Australia and New Zealand will require holders to submit their Electronic Travel Authorization. 

Out of the 188 countries, Canadian passport holders can gain entry without visas, 45 of them will require that you apply for a visa on arrival. The documents that will be required includes:

  • Passport (with at least, 6 months validity and at least, one blank visa page)
  • A proof showing your willingness to leave the country after your visa expires(a return travel ticket)
  • Proof of accommodation in your new visiting country (this could be a letter of invitation from a host or a hotel reservation).
  • You must demonstrate financial capability enough to cover your stay (bank statements)

However, you won’t necessarily have to show these documents always. The demand for these requirements vary by country and for some, your passport and flight ticket is enough. 

Can I get into any country with a Canadian Passport?

Canadian passport holders are required to obtain visas before they can get into about 41 destinations in the world. Some countries where visas are required for Canadian passport holders include:

  • India
  • Turkey
  • China
  • Russia, etc.

This report shows that there are a total of 138 countries Canadians can visit without a visa, 45 Canada visas on arrival countries, and 5 eTA destinations.

]]> Alberta is one of the Prairies Provinces in Western Canada. The province has a natural resource portfolio that consists of natural gas, minerals, coal, oil, and Alberta oil sands. This makes Alberta one of the fastest-growing economies in Canada and a popular destination for newcomers, who come looking for job opportunities through the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program.

Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program

The Alberta Immigration Nominee Program (AINP) is one of Canada’s Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs). It is an economic immigration program that allows immigrants Permanent Residence (PR) in Alberta. Usually, these immigrants are foreign workers, students, or entrepreneurs interested in settling in Alberta permanently.

The program is designed for candidates with the skills, abilities, and experiences to benefit and fill labour shortages in Alberta. Or candidates looking to buy or start a new business in the province.

The Alberta government regulates this program in partnership with the IRCC. To get nominated, candidates must apply for a nomination to the provincial government. If nominated, then the candidate may apply for PR to the federal government together with their spouse or children.

Alberta Immigration Nominee Program (AINP) Streams

This program offers five streams:

  • Opportunity stream
  • International graduate entrepreneur immigration stream
  • Express Entry stream
  • Foreign graduate startup visa stream
  • Self-employed farmer stream

Opportunity Stream:

This stream offers an immigration pathway to foreign workers in the province. It requires that a candidate must already have a job offer in the province to be eligible.

International Graduate Entrepreneur Immigration Stream (IGEIS)

This stream is for students who have already completed post-secondary education in the province and wish to reside permanently. This can either be by buying or starting a new business.

Express Entry stream

This stream requires that candidates must have an active Express Entry profile. They will be invited through their accounts with specific selection criteria. One of the major advantages of this stream is that candidates receive an additional 600 Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) points. Also, they receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for PR. 

Foreign Graduate Start-up Visa Stream (FGSVS)

This stream is specifically designed for candidates who have completed post-secondary education outside Canada but wish to reside in the province. To be eligible, candidates must be interested in starting a new business in the province.

Self-Employed Farmer stream

This stream is for farmers who possess enough experience to buy or start a farm in the province. 

What are the Eligibility Criteria of the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)

The Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP) depends on the stream a candidate chooses. However, here is a list of the general criteria:

  • A valid work permit earned through Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)
  • Proof and status of temporary resident in Canada
  • Proof of work in an occupation accepted by AINP
  • Work experience for the job you want to apply
  • Proof of funds to settle in Canada
  • Language test results. Applicants must score at least 5 for CLB and NCLC (7 is required in some cases)
  • The language skill results must be from IELTS/CELPIP/TEF
  • Educational certificate from a post-secondary institution, a diploma for two years, or an undergraduate/graduate degree
  • Full-time work experience of 12 months in the province within the past 18 months. Or full-time work experience in 24 months in Canada within the past 30 months

However, a valid work permit is not needed in these cases:

  • When applicants have an LMIA exempt occupation
  • When applicants have a post-graduation work permit.

What Documents are Required to Apply Under the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program?

  • A valid passport
  • Fully completed and signed application form
  • Work permit
  • Birth certificate
  • Address proof
  • Marriage certificate (if present)
  • Police clearance certificate
  • Language skills results (For English – IELTS/CELPIP and French – TCF/TEF)
  • Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)
  • Educational Credential Assessment (ECA)
  • Employee reference letter (if needed)
  • Employment letter
  • Statement of Earnings
  • Proof of funds

How to apply for the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program

Here is how you can apply for the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program:

  • Check the eligibility criteria
  • Create an Express Entry profile
  • Ensure you submit the necessary documents to get your application to reach the Express Entry pool
  • The Alberta province reviews your profile at this point
  • Get the Notification of Interest (NOI) from Alberta
  • Submit a complete application much earlier before the deadline
  • Check your Express Entry account to receive the notification and accept the provincial nomination and also get the 600 additional points added to your CRS score – the minimum CRS score for Alberta is 300
  • Submit the Expression of Interest (EOI)
  • If your application is accepted, you will be selected from the Express Entry draw
  • Receive the ITA

What is the Cost of Applying to the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program?

Although this is dependent on the stream you are applying under, the general cost of the AINP is broken down below:

  • Right of Permanent Residence Fee –  CAD 500
  • Processing Fee –  CAD 550
  • Dependent child –  CAD 225

You must visit their website for updates.

How Long Does it Take to get Alberta’s PNP?

This will depend on the stream you have applied under:

  • The skilled worker category – one month
  • The semi-skilled worker category – four months
  • International Graduates Category – one month
  • Engineering Occupations Category – one month
  • Compulsory and Optional Trades Category – one month

]]> Saskatchewan is one of Canada’s fastest-growing provinces located in Western Canada’s parade region. It is rich in farmlands and is known as Canada’s hub of agricultural production. The province is home to two major cities in Canada, Saskatoon and Regina, and has an immigration pathway, the Saskatchewan Provincial Nominee Program (SINP).

Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP)

The Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program is one of Canada’s immigration pathways. It is a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) that enables foreign individuals to become permanent residents of Canada.

This program welcomes immigrants who possess the skills and abilities to work in sectors with labour shortages in the province. It allows the province to nominate non-Canadian applicants with the required skills and experiences for Permanent Residence (PR).

What are the Programs Under SINP?

The Saskatchewan province welcomes immigrants in the following categories:

  • Saskatchewan International Skilled Worker
  • Worker with Saskatchewan Work Experience
  • Saskatchewan Entrepreneur and Farm

Worker With Saskatchewan Work Experience 

This category is for international workers who already have temporary residence in the province. It is also for foreign nationals with a valid work permit or a temporary work permit. This category is divided into the following streams:

  • Skilled Worker with Existing Work Permit
  • Semi-skilled Agriculture Worker with Existing Work Permit
  • Long-haul Truck Driver Project
  • Students
  • Hospitality Sector Pilot Project

Saskatchewan International Skilled Worker

This category targets foreign nationals who possess the required skills and abilities to fill the gaps in the province’s labour market. To qualify, applicants must meet the needs of the desired occupation on the Saskatchewan list.

This category is divided into the following streams:

  • Employment offer
  • Saskatchewan Express Entry
  • Occupation in demand

Saskatchewan Entrepreneur and Farm

This is the business immigration category. It is strategically designed for immigrants who want to start farming in the province. It operates on three different streams targeting foreigners who are willing to create a new business in the province:

  • Farm owner and operator
  • Entrepreneur
  • International graduate entrepreneur

Eligibility Criteria to Apply for Saskatchewan PNP

Below is the eligibility criteria to apply for SINP: 

  • Express Entry Profile Number and Job Seeker Validation Code
  • Proof of Saskatchewan study and/or work experience
  • Language test results such as International English Language Testing System (IELTS, GENERAL TRAINING) – Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program (CELPIP). And Test d ‘evaluation de français (TEF) for French
  • Post-secondary education qualification or training
  • Full-time work experience for one year in NOC I, A or B Skilled Occupations
  • Proof of employment and reference letter
  • Proof of professional status or licensure

Documents Required to Apply for Saskatchewan Provincial Nomination Program

The documents needed for the Saskatchewan Provincial Nomination Program may differ across the different categories and streams. However, here is a general list:

  • Valid passport
  • Medical test results
  • Identity proof
  • Bank statements
  • Proof of funds
  • Police Clearance Certificate or evidence of character
  • Educational Credential Assessment (ECA)
  • Language proficiency test results
  • Settlement plan
  • Letter of employment
  • Evidence of past work experience
  • Marriage certificate – if your spouse is immigrating with you
  • Dependent Children Information Certificate – if your dependent child is accompanying you.

Also, you will need to score at least 60 out of 110 points on the SINP point assessment grid. These points are based on the following:

  • Your age
  • Communication skills
  • Employment
  • Qualification
  • Blood relative in the province
  • Job offer letter
  • Adaptability

How to Apply for the Saskatchewan Provincial Nomination Program

There are two ways you can apply for the SINP; the Express Entry system and the non-Express Entry system:

First, you have to submit your Expression of Interest (EOI). If accepted, then apply for SINP online and critically follow all application procedures. 

To apply through the Express Entry system:

  • Create an Express Entry Profile
  • Select Saskatchewan as your preferred residence
  • Create a profile on the online profile of SINP
  • Complete all formalities and wait to be asked for your documents
  • Upload your documents in PDF format
  • If accepted, you will receive a provincial nomination
  • Then apply for a permanent residence
  • Get your visa and move to Canada!

What is the Cost of SINP?

The Saskatchewan Provincial Nominee Program costs a non-refundable fee of $350re are other fees you might need to pay:

Dependent child – $225.

]]> Are you planning to move to Canada and get a permanent residence (PR)? The Provincial Programs (PNP) in Canada is a feasible way to move. Provinces and territories can nominate individuals for a Canadian PR. In this article, you will learn the most popular provincial nominee programs in Canada This article discusses the popular PNPs in Canada.

What are Provincial Nominee Programs?

The Provincial Nomination Programs (PNPs) are Canada’s leading immigration pathway that enables individuals to obtain permanent residence in Canada. 

It authorizes provinces and territories in Canada to nominate individuals looking to relocate to Canada. Nominated individuals must have to reside in the provinces that nominated them.

Basically, there are reasons why applicants would choose the Provincial Nominee Program over other immigration pathways:

  • Applicant already temporarily reside in the province or territory. They might have either finished working or studying temporarily and are then eligible 
  • They received a job offer in the territory or province
  • The applicant did not have enough CRS points to get an Invitation to Apply (ITA) in the Express Entry pool. A provincial nomination application gives them an additional 600 points to gain them the ITA.

7 Popular Provincial Nominee Programs in Canada You Need to Know

There are roughly over 80 different provincial nominee programs in Canada. However, each of the thirteen territories and provinces in Canada regulates its PNPs with numerous streams. Below are seven popular PNPs in Canada you need to know:

1. Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP)

The Ontario immigrant nominee program is one of the most diversified and active in Canada’s PNPs. It is designed for graduates, skilled workers, and business people to plan their immigration to Ontario.

The program is the province’s economic immigration program. Ontario works with the Government of Canada through Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). OINP is open to international students, workers, and anyone with the right skills, education, and experiences that Ontario’s economy needs. 

Meanwhile, there are other ways you may qualify for the OINP nomination:

  • Employer job offer category: this is for foreign workers who already gained a permanent job offer from an Ontario employer
  • Human capital category: this is for workers who possess valuable work experiences, language skills, and education qualifications.
  • Business category: this is designed for entrepreneurs who either want to buy or start a new business in Ontario.

2. British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BCPNP)

The BCPNP offers candidates a wide range of stream and category options for graduates, entrepreneurs, and workers. It aligns with the categories in the federal Express Entry immigration system in Canada.

This program offers permanent residence to candidates who will work in sectors with labor shortages. It operates on several streams among two categories:

British Columbia Skills Immigration

  • British Columbia Entrepreneur Immigration
  • BC Skills Immigration

British Columbia Skilled Worker

  • BC Healthcare Professional
  • British Columbia International Graduate
  • British Columbia International Post-Graduate
  • BC Entry Level and Semi-Skilled Worker (ELSS)

This British Columbia Skills Immigration category targets candidates who have employment futures in BC and whose skills will benefit the province.

3. Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP)

The Alberta immigration nominee program is one of the most popular PNPs in Canada for new immigrants. The province is one of Canada’s three Prairie provinces in Western Canada. It offers PNP streams for both Express Entry and non-Express Entry candidates. AINP is open to graduates, workers, and entrepreneurs who possess the abilities and skills to fill labour shortages in the province.

One of the significant advantages of this stream is its minimum CRS score of 300. This ensures that more candidates in the pool have greater chances to obtain provincial notifications of interest.

It currently accepts applications under these five streams:

  • The self-employed farmer stream
  • The international graduate entrepreneur immigration stream
  • The foreign graduate start-up visa stream
  • Alberta opportunity stream
  • Express entry

4. Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program

If you have a connection in Manitoba, this nominee program might be the ideal alternative for you. You’re considered to have a connection if you studied, worked, or have a relative or friend in the province. Participating in any of the province’s strategic recruitment initiatives is also considered to be a connection.

Whichever connection you have gives you a better chance of receiving an ITA. Also, to be eligible to apply, you must have a one-year work experience. This work experience must be in one of the Manitoba In-Demand Occupation List.

MPNP is opened to skilled workers, recent graduates, agricultural entrepreneurs, and workers who have the experience to work in sectors with labour shortages.

The province regulates numerous immigration streams among four categories:

  • Skilled Workers in Manitoba (SWM)
  • Skilled Workers Overseas (SWO)
  • International Education Stream (IES)
  • Business Investor Stream (BIS)

5. Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program

The Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program welcomes workers in in-demand occupations as well as Express Entry candidates.

It performs on two streams:

  • The Saskatchewan Express Entry stream
  • The Saskatchewan Occupation In-Demand stream

The Express Entry stream requires that candidates must have active Express Entry profiles. On the other hand, the Occupation in-demand stream does not. Instead, it requires that applicants must have at least one year of work experience. This experience could be in any of the positions on the in-demand occupation list.

6. Nova Scotia Nominee Program

This nominee program is a popular destination for newcomers. Aligned with Express Entry, it invites a particular National Occupation Classification (NOC) every year. This means that it does not require that you have a job offer to be eligible to apply.

It is designed to fill in labour market gaps. It will require you to be in the Express Entry pool, and upon receiving your ITA, you will be required to submit your application.

]]> The Canadian Experience Class (CEC) is an immigration program that allows temporary foreign workers who have worked for at least one year to gain permanent resident status in Canada.

It aims to recognize the deep pool of talented workers already working in the country, retain and grant them permanent residence in Canada.

What is the Processing Time for the Canadian Experience Class?

The processing time for the Canadian Experience Class is often within 3-4 months. Applicants will receive confirmation of the permanent residence document once approved.

How Many Points Do You Need for the Canadian Experience Class?

To apply for the Canadian Experience Class, you must gain at least 67 points to qualify for Express Entry. Also, you need at least the least CRS score for a draw to be invited to reside in Canada permanently.

Eligibility Requirements for Canada Experience Class

These are the requirements you must meet to qualify for the Canadian Experience Class.

Language Eligibility

You will be required to meet the language ability levels needed for your job. These abilities include:

  • Writing
  • Reading
  • Listening
  • Speaking

You are required to meet the minimum language level of either of the following for those abilities:

  • Canadian Language Benchmark 7 for NOC 0 or A jobs
  • Canadian Language Benchmark 7 for NOC B job

Also, Canadian Experience Class candidates may take any of the following IRCC-approved language tests:

  • CELPIP-General (English)
  • IELTS-General (English)
  • TEF (French)
  • TCF (French)

Your language test result is valid for only two years after the date of the test result. It must be valid during the time you are applying for permanent residence.

Work Experience

You must have gained at least one year of skilled work experience in Canada while under temporary status. The experience must be in the last three years before you apply. There are specific ways you can meet this requirement:

  • Full-time job: 30 hours per week for 12 months (1,560 hours)
  • Part-time job: 15 hours per week for 24 months (1,560 hours).

Also, you can work as many part-time jobs as necessary to meet the requirement. You must have the authorization to work, and your skilled work must be paid. Volunteers and unpaid internships do not count.

Your work experience must be in either of these National Occupation Classification (NOC) levels:

  • Managerial jobs (skill level 0)
  • Professional jobs (skill type A)
  • Technical jobs and skilled trades (skill type B)

Typically, your work experience can be in one or more NOC 0, A, or B jobs. Self-employment and student work experience do not count.

Education

There are no education requirements for the Canadian Experience Class. However, your educational qualifications can improve your rank in the Express Entry pool. Here are ways to do this:

If you schooled in Canada, you could gain extra points for a certificate; diploma, or degree from a Canadian:

  • High school
  • Post-secondary institution

Also, if you have foreign education, you can get points for a completed foreign credential.

Admissibility

You must be eligible to live in Canada and plan on living outside the province of Quebec. Meanwhile, if you are a Provincial Nominee, you must live in the province that nominated you.

Is the Canadian Experience Class Different From Express Entry?

While Canadian Experience Entry is one of the three Federal High Skilled immigration programs managed by Express Entry. Express Entry is a point-based system that controls the immigration applications for the there Federal High Skilled Programs:

  • Canadian Experience Class
  • Federal Skilled Worker Program
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program.

It allows the IRCC to invite competent candidates to apply for permanent residence in Canada.

]]> The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) is an immigration program that allows applicants to live in a specific province, contribute to the province’s economy and gain permanent residence. It is an alternative to the Canadian Federal Skilled Worker visa.

It allows provinces and territories to meet the immigration needs of applicants. They do this by helping them fill in-demand jobs and meet labour shortages in the province.

How Does the Provincial Nominee Program in Canada Work?

Applicants with eligible Provincial Nomination will have the chance of getting an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

The Provincial Nominee Program in Canada offers these applicants who desire to reside in a specific Canadian province an immigration pathway permanently.

However, these provinces and territories have unique economic and demographic requirements applicants will need to meet. Also, each province or territory has its own streams and requirements to target specific groups of immigrants. The groups may be any of:

  • Business people
  • Skilled workers
  • Semi-skilled workers
  • Students

Meanwhile, most PNPs will require that applicants already have some connection in the province. The connection involves having previously studied or worked in the province. If not, they must have a job offer from an employer in the province for a job visa.

However, some PNPs do not require any previous connections. You have to apply directly and meet all requirements to gain enough CRS scores. The CRS score qualifies you to receive the Invitation to Apply (ITA). Once your application reaches the Express Entry pool, you will gain an additional CRS score when applying for a PR visa. The minimum CRS score for Express Entry is 692.

Ideally, the program is specifically designed for candidates who cannot meet the minimum criteria to qualify for the PR visa of Canada. Also, candidates who cannot score enough CRS to receive an Invitation to Apply to the Express Entry pool.

How Can I Get PNP in Canada?

To get the PNP in Canada, you must meet the set requirements for applicants. Although the documents required for the PNP depends on the specific qualifying standards, there are some essential documents required:

  • Valid passport
  • Recent color photographs
  • Identity proof
  • General application form of the principal applicant
  • Attested documents by the spouse and other dependents
  • Documents supporting the spouse and other dependents
  • Language skills results
  • Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) report
  • Police clearance
  • Medical certificate
  • Proof of fund.

Provincial Nominee Programs Process in Canada

There are two ways you can apply for the PNP in Canada:

  • Express Entry System
  • Non-Express Entry System

The Express Entry System can be Processed in Two Ways:

The applicants create an Express Entry Profile and reach the Express Entry pool after submitting the necessary documents. The different provinces and territories then pick their suitable applicants, and they are sent the Notification of Interest (NOI). Applicants who receive the NOI can send their Expression of Interest (EOI) to the Express Entry.

In the second way to the Express Entry System, the applicants will check for suitable PNPs and apply for the nomination. They can create the Express Entry profile when the province or territory they applied to agrees for a nomination. There they can show that they have the provincial nomination.

Non- Express Entry System:

The Non-Express Entry System is a paper-based application. The applicants will have to select a suitable province whose eligibility requirements they best meet. They submit the EOI under the non-Entry Express stream. Their application will be assessed based on the point system of the particular territory or province. The selected applicants will receive a nomination letter. Afterward, they must submit a paper application for Permanent Residency (PR) visa to the Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC).

Which PNP is Best in Canada?

All PNP in Canada is great and unique. The best and easiest will depend on the applicants and their areas of expertise. However, here are the best three:

Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP):

This program offers numerous tech job opportunities. It consists of categories to easily allow skilled workers, graduates, and business owners to migrate to Canada.

To apply to this program, you must have a minimum CRS score of 400 points and higher. Also, the program offers streams for both French-speaking Skilled Workers and Skilled Trades Stream.

Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP):

This PNP program offers a broad range of occupations. Applicants who have had difficulties under the Express Entry system will find this program much more accessible.

To qualify, you have to:

  • Have at most minor, one-year work experience in any jobs in Saskatchewan’s in-demand occupations
  • Have completed your education up to post-secondary level
  • Be proficient in either English or French language

Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP):

This program offers various options to entrepreneurs, skilled workers, international graduates, and temporary foreign workers.

To qualify to apply, you must have an active Express Entry profile. Also, you must have a job offer from an employer in the province. There’s another option for candidates who have experience in any of the in-demand occupations in the province. These options are Categories A and B, respectively.
